What You Actually Do

Quick meaning, no fluff.

Simple Definition

A data analyst collects, cleans, studies, and explains data so people can make better decisions.

In Plain Language

You turn messy numbers into a story the business can use. You help teams move from confusion to clarity.

What Makes You Useful

You do not just make charts. You find patterns, explain causes, and support action.

Mindset

Think like someone standing in a busy market, a township transport route, or a growing fintech app: there is movement everywhere, but the job is to spot signal, not noise. Data analysis is about seeing the pattern beneath the pressure.

The Six Ways You Read Data

These came directly from the PDF and were simplified for memory.

01 Descriptive Analysis
▾

Question: What happened?

  • Summarises past data.
  • Uses averages, totals, distributions, charts.
  • Great for monthly sales, user counts, completions.
02 Diagnostic Analysis
▾

Question: Why did it happen?

  • Looks for drivers behind a result.
  • Compares variables and patterns.
  • Useful when sales drop or engagement falls.
03 Predictive Analysis
▾

Question: What may happen next?

  • Uses trends and models to estimate future outcomes.
  • Good for demand, churn, risk, or growth forecasts.
  • Think probability, not prophecy.
04 Causal Analysis
▾

Question: What caused the change?

  • Separates cause from simple correlation.
  • Often uses regression, tests, or experiments.
  • Useful for pricing, ads, onboarding, or product changes.
05 Exploratory Data Analysis
▾

Question: What patterns are hiding here?

  • Used early when a dataset is still unfamiliar.
  • Looks for outliers, trends, and strange behavior.
  • Often the place where strong questions begin.
06 Prescriptive Analysis
▾

Question: What should we do now?

  • Turns findings into recommended action.
  • Useful for strategy, optimization, and next steps.
  • This is where data meets decision-making.

Your Workflow In Motion

This follows the process from the PDF, shortened for mobile recall.

Collect Clean Transform Explore Analyse Visualise Report Recommend

Your daily loop is simple: bring the data in, make it trustworthy, look for meaning, then help the business act. A good analyst does not stop at charts. You stop when the answer is clear enough to use.

Collect + Clean

Gather data from databases, spreadsheets, APIs, or surveys. Fix duplicates, missing values, and inconsistent labels.

Explore + Analyse

Check structure, spot outliers, test ideas, compare groups, and build confidence in the patterns you find.

Report + Recommend

Show the insight, explain the business effect, then suggest the next action in plain language.

Career Path

Also based on the PDF, with clearer language.

Stage 1 Junior Data Analyst
▾
  • Main work: collect, clean, and do basic analysis.
  • Focus: build confidence with tools and data quality.
  • Grow by becoming reliable.
Stage 2 Intermediate Data Analyst
▾
  • Main work: deeper analysis and cross-team collaboration.
  • Focus: explain trends and answer business questions.
  • Grow by becoming insightful.
Stage 3 Senior Data Analyst
▾
  • Main work: lead projects and guide other analysts.
  • Focus: strategy, predictive work, and strong judgment.
  • Grow by becoming trusted.

Remember The Roots

The manual part that keeps the work grounded.

African Context

Data work is not only for big foreign tech firms. It matters in African realities too: learner platforms, township logistics, mobile lending, health outreach, retail footfall, agri supply chains, and public service delivery. The same analyst mindset works everywhere: see the pattern, protect the truth, and help people decide better.

Quick Examples
  • Which learning modules keep learners engaged?
  • Which route or branch has the biggest drop in sales?
  • Which customer group converts best after onboarding?
  • Where are the biggest drop-offs in a mobile journey?
Your Quiet Creed

Clean the signal. Respect the people behind the numbers. Tell the truth clearly.
